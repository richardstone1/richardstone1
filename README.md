# Welcome

I've spent more than four decades designing, debugging, and supporting embedded and software systems. Most of that work was performed for employers and customers, and remains proprietary.

### My Engineering Philosophy

My engineering process is iterative: follow a plausible path, test it against reality, measure what happens, find what I missed, refine the question, and repeat. 

The more experience I've gained, the more I realize my greatest power is reviewing my work and asking more questions before I'm done. Then the other fork is knowing when to say "enough" and finalize.

### Notes on the Craft

After four decades, I’ve found that the best engineering isn't about knowing the answer—it's about knowing how to ask the right questions. I treat every project as an opportunity to pressure-test my own intuition against the reality of the hardware. 

Experience has taught me that the most powerful solutions are often the simplest ones I overlooked years ago. My current process is a constant cycle of unlearning assumptions to return to the core physics of the problem. The result is often less code, more reliability, and a much deeper understanding of the system's limitations.

### This Repository

This GitHub contains personal projects and experiments that I can share publicly. They reflect the same engineering approach I've used throughout my career: understand the system, gather data, test assumptions, measure results, and document the findings.

* Embedded firmware
